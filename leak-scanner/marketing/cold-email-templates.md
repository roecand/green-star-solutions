# Cold email templates

Rules: short, specific, one CTA, no attachments, no fake urgency. Send from
Robert personally. The scanner's admin panel has a copy-paste opener per lead.

## 1. The permission opener (default — used by admin "copy email opener")
**Subject:** quick thing about {{website}}

Hey {{first_name}},

I ran {{website}} through our Revenue Leak Scanner and found a few things
that may be costing you calls — it scored {{score}}/100. Want me to send the
report? It's free and takes 2 minutes to read.

— Robert, Greenstar Solutions

## 2. The one-finding tease
**Subject:** your phone number is hiding

Hey {{first_name}},

Random but useful: I was looking at {{business_name}}'s site and noticed
{{specific_finding, e.g. "your phone number doesn't show up until the contact
page"}}. On mobile that usually means ready-to-call customers give up.

I run a free scanner that checks for 50 leaks like this. Want the full
report for your site?

— Robert

## 3. The report delivery (after a yes)
**Subject:** your Revenue Leak Report ({{score}}/100)

Here it is: {{report_link}}

Two things worth your attention first:
1. {{top_leak_1}}
2. {{top_leak_2}}

Both are fixable in days, not months. If you want, I'll walk you through the
whole report on a 20-minute call — no charge, no pressure: {{booking_link}}

— Robert

## 4. The follow-up (3–4 days after report, no reply)
**Subject:** re: your Revenue Leak Report

Hey {{first_name}} — did you get a chance to look at the report?

The one I'd fix first is {{top_leak_1}}. {{one_sentence_why}}.

Happy to point you in the right direction even if you fix it yourself.

— Robert

## 5. The re-scan nudge (30+ days later)
**Subject:** did anything change?

Hey {{first_name}},

A month ago {{website}} scored {{score}}/100 on our leak scan. If you've
made changes since, I'll re-run it for free so you can see the score move.
And if not — the three fixes from the report are still sitting there.

— Robert
